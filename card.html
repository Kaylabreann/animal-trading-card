Mantis Shrimp

Mantis Shrimp have the best eyes in the world! The Mantis Shrimps independantly moving eyes have 12-16 color receptors, and can detect ultra violet and polarised light.

  • Scientific Name: Stomatopoda
  • Average Length: 4 to 8 inches long
  • Average Lifespan: Up to 20 years
  • Habitat: Indian and Pacific Oceans

Neither Mantis nor Shrimp - the Mantis Shrimp is one of the oldest species on Earth. There are approximately 450 Mantis Shrimp species covered in vibrant colors. Their punches move about the same velocity of a .22 caliber rifle. These punches can generate temperatures as hot as the sun in surrounding water in a split second.
